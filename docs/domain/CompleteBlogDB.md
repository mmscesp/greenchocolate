- Not coffeeshops (no public access, no tourist model)
- Not dispensaries (no medical framework in most regions)

- Never follow a street promoter

- No photos. Ever. Of anything. Of anyone.
- No videos. No stories. No check-ins.
- Don't ask other members personal questions
- Don't discuss prices or quantities loudly
- Respect the space like someone's home — because legally, that's closer to what it is

- No house rules posted or explained

- Stay calm
- Leave the situation if you feel unsafe
- Don't argue with anyone — just leave

## ARTICLE 5

**Title:** Spain's Cannabis Laws: What Tourists Actually Need to Know

**Tag:** Legal Guide

**Slug:** `/en/guides/spain-cannabis-laws-tourists`

**Read time:** 10 min

**Target keywords:** is cannabis legal in spain, spain cannabis law tourists, cannabis fines spain, public consumption cannabis spain

**Purpose:** This is the article linked from the Reality Check's "Read the full legal landscape" CTA. It's your E-E-A-T authority builder. Cite sources. Be accurate. Don't give legal advice — give legal context.

**Outline:**

**Section 1 — The Short Answer**
- Cannabis is NOT legal in Spain
- Private consumption in private spaces is decriminalized (not legalized)
- Public consumption/possession is an administrative offense
- There is no national regulatory framework for CSCs

**Section 2 — Private vs Public: The Line That Matters**
- Private consumption in a private residence: not penalized
- Private consumption in a CSC: grey zone (see below)
- Public consumption anywhere: administrative fine
- Public possession: administrative fine
- The fine range: €601 to €30,000 (Organic Law 4/2015, Art. 36.16)

**Section 3 — What "Grey Zone" Actually Means**
- No law says CSCs are legal
- No law says CSCs are illegal
- Courts have ruled case-by-case
- Supreme Court has treated organized distribution within associations as potentially meeting trafficking elements (Art. 368 Código Penal)
- Catalonia tried to create a regulatory framework (Law 13/2017) — struck down as unconstitutional (STC 100/2018)
- The result: CSCs exist in a space of legal uncertainty

**Section 4 — What This Means for Tourists Specifically**
- You are subject to Spanish law while in Spain
- Being a tourist does not give you special status or protection
- If you consume in public, you can be fined regardless of nationality
- If you join a CSC as a member, you're participating in the grey zone
- Our recommendation: understand the risks, make informed decisions

**Section 5 — The Fine Process**
- Administrative fines, not criminal charges (for simple public consumption)
- Issued by police on the spot with official documentation
- Payment process and timelines
- Appeals exist but are complex
- Anyone asking you to pay cash on the street without paperwork is scamming you

**Section 6 — What We Are NOT Saying**
- We are not lawyers
- This is not legal advice
- This is a factual overview of publicly available legal information
- For personal legal situations, consult a Spanish lawyer
- Sources cited at bottom of article

**Section 7 — Source List**
- Organic Law 4/2015 (Ley Orgánica 4/2015)
- Código Penal Art. 368
- STC 100/2018 (Constitutional Court ruling on Catalonia's law)
- EUDA (European Union Drugs Agency) Spain country profile
