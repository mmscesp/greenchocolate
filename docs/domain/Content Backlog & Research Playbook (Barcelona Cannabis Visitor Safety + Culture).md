## 2) Our repeatable research method (how we generate winning titles + outlines)

### Step A — Pick a “pillar” (so we build topical authority)
We maintain 5 pillars (each pillar gets clusters of related posts):
1. **Legality & policy reality** (public vs private, fines, what changed recently)
2. **Harm reduction** (edibles, vaping, overconsumption, mixing substances)
3. **Scams & misinformation** (DM scams, fake storefront claims, misleading “legal highs”)
4. **Etiquette & culture** (respect, privacy, neighborhood impact)
5. **Health system & safety resources** (what to do if unwell; drug checking; driving risk)

### Step B — SERP reconnaissance (10–20 minutes per post)
Goal: confirm the query is real and identify the “content gap” we can own.

Checklist:
- Google the primary query in Incognito (and optionally Spanish query variants).
- Capture:
  - Top 5–10 ranking pages (what angles do they take?)
  - “People Also Ask” questions
  - Related searches
  - Any obvious **affiliate spam** or **outdated posts** (our opportunity)
- Decide the post’s angle:
  - **Correct misinformation**
  - **Update outdated info**
  - **Add safety detail missing everywhere**
  - **Translate official policy into plain language**

### Step C — Source-of-truth collection (primary sources first)
For claims about law/enforcement/medical frameworks, we prefer:
- **BOE (Boletín Oficial del Estado)** for legal orders/decrees
- **EUDA / EMCDDA-style references** for policy summaries
- **Ajuntament de Barcelona / Guardia Urbana** for city operations/inspections
- **AEMPS** for medical cannabis preparation frameworks and official positions
- **AESAN** for food safety alerts (edibles/cannabinoid sweets)
- **DGT** for driving + roadside drug enforcement info

Rule: If we can’t back a claim with a strong source, we either:
- remove it, or
- label it clearly as anecdotal / community-reported.

### Step D — Community intelligence (what people actually struggle with)
We use community spaces (e.g., r/BarcelonaEnts) to find:
- recurring questions,
- repeated “failure modes” (bad tourist behavior, panic on edibles, scams),
- confusing terms (HHC/THCP/etc.),
- etiquette norms.

We treat this as:
- **question mining** and **myth detection**,
not as a definitive source of legal truth.

### Step E — Title engineering (intent + CTR + trust)
We craft titles that:
- match high-intent phrasing (“Is X legal…”, “Fines…”, “What to do if…”),
- include the year when time-sensitive,
- promise clarity (“explained”, “checklist”, “myths vs reality”),
- remain compliance-safe (no “how to get” framing).

Title patterns we reuse:
- “Is X legal in [City] in [Year]?”
- “X vs Y: what’s safer / what’s the difference?”
- “Checklist: X red flags / do’s and don’ts”
- “Explained: what changed in [month/year] and why it matters”
- “What to do if… (panic, too high, bad reaction)”

### Step F — Outline for “helpfulness signals” (E-E-A-T)
Every post should contain:
- a plain-language **answer box** near the top,
- a **what changed recently** box (if applicable),
- at least one **concrete number/date** (only if verified),
- a **misconceptions** section,
- a **practical checklist** users can act on safely.

### Step G — Internal linking & cluster building
Every new post must link to:
- 1 pillar page (Legal Reality / Harm Reduction / Scam Avoidance / Etiquette / Safety Resources)
- 2–4 sibling posts (same cluster)
- 1 “what to do if unwell” safety page (when relevant)

## 3) Editorial guardrails (what we will NOT publish)
We do not publish:
- “best clubs”, “where to join”, “how to get invited”, “membership walkthroughs”
- where to buy cannabis or how to transport it
- tips to avoid police detection or evade enforcement
- referral/DM instructions

We do publish:
- legality context (with citations),
- public health + harm reduction,
- etiquette/respect culture,
- scam warnings,
- driving safety & general travel safety.

## 4) SEO mechanics we use (simple + effective)
- Primary keyword in:
  - H1 title
  - first 100 words
  - one H2
  - meta title/description (in the codebase where applicable)
- Add “freshness hooks”:
  - year in title for legal/policy posts
  - “last updated” timestamp on page
- Use FAQ sections (good for long-tail):
  - Answer short questions in 2–4 sentences each.
- Add a glossary (HHC, THCP, THCA, “administrative fine”, etc.)
- Avoid thin content: aim for “complete guide” depth per query.
