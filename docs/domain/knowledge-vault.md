## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### What This Document Contains

This knowledge vault represents a comprehensive synthesis of research gathered from BarcelonaEnts subreddit (the primary community knowledge base), official Spanish legal documentation, enforcement reports, and authoritative cannabis industry sources. The document serves as the foundational reference for creating a market-leading cannabis social club platform in Spain.

### Core Positioning Statement

Barcelona's cannabis scene operates in a carefully balanced legal grey zone. Unlike Amsterdam's open coffeeshops, Barcelona's cannabis social clubs function as private, non-profit associations where members collectively fund cultivation and share cannabis among themselves. This distinction is fundamental to understanding the market, the legal risks, and the operational model that any successful platform must navigate.

### Key Strategic Insights

The Barcelona cannabis market presents unique opportunities and challenges that differ significantly from other European cannabis destinations. The community-driven nature of the scene, combined with increasing regulatory scrutiny, creates demand for trustworthy information platforms that prioritize safety, education, and harm reduction over promotional content.

Success in this market requires:

## 2. LEGAL FRAMEWORK: SPAIN & BARCELONA

### 2.1 Spanish Cannabis Law Overview

Spain's relationship with cannabis operates on multiple legal levels that create the unique grey zone in which cannabis social clubs function.

#### Federal Criminal Law

Under the Spanish Penal Code, drug trafficking and cultivation for trafficking remain criminal offenses. However, the law distinguishes between trafficking and personal consumption in private settings. This distinction forms the legal foundation upon which cannabis social clubs operate.

#### Organic Law 4/2015 (Public Safety)

This legislation governs public consumption and possession, treating these as administrative infractions rather than criminal offenses. The financial penalties for public consumption or possession are significant and represent the primary legal risk for individuals:

| Infraction Type | Penalty Range | Notes |
|-----------------|---------------|-------|
| Minor public possession/consumption | €100–300 | Rarely applied to small amounts |
| Standard public possession/consumption | €601–30,000 | Most common penalty range |
| Aggravated circumstances | Up to €30,000+ | Factors include proximity to minors, recidivism, public nuisance |

#### Constitutional Court Ruling (2018)

The Catalan Parliament attempted to establish a specific regulatory framework for cannabis associations, but Spain's Constitutional Court overturned this legislation in 2018. The Court ruled that the regulation of narcotics falls under exclusive federal (state) competence, not regional authority. This ruling cemented the legal uncertainty that defines the current landscape.

#### Supreme Court Jurisprudence

Spain's Supreme Court has progressively narrowed the scope of what can be defended as "shared consumption" versus organized supply. Key precedents establish that:
- Scale of operations matters significantly
- Open membership policies can be interpreted as trafficking
- Non-profit status does not automatically provide legal protection
- The appearance of commercial activity creates liability

### 2.2 The Grey Zone Explained

#### What Is Tolerated

In practice, Barcelona and Catalonia have historically exercised tolerance toward properly structured cannabis social clubs operating under specific conditions:

- **Private membership**: Clubs that maintain closed membership lists and verify member identity
- **Non-commercial operation**: No direct sales; members make "donations" to cover cultivation costs
- **Private consumption**: All consumption occurs within the club's private premises
- **Small-scale**: Operations remain modest in size and visibility

#### What Is NOT Tolerated

The authorities actively target:
- **Public-facing retail**: Any operation that appears to sell cannabis to the public
- **Tourist-oriented marketing**: Clubs that actively recruit visitors as members
- **Street-level dealing**: Any distribution outside the private club setting
- **Open membership**: Clubs that don't verify residency or membership criteria
- **Nuisance behavior**: Complaints from neighbors, visible public consumption

### 2.3 2025 Legal Updates

#### Orden SND/380/2025 (April 2025)

This Royal Decree added multiple semi-synthetic cannabinoids to the controlled substances list, with direct implications for product safety and legal compliance:

**Controlled Substances Added:**
- Hexahydrocannabinol (HHC)
- HHC-O (HHC acetate)
- THCP (Tetrahydrocannabiphorol) variants
- THC-O (THC acetate)
- THCA (Tetrahydrocannabinolic acid)

**Effective Date:** April 23, 2025

**Platform Implication:** Any content about "legal high" alternatives must explicitly warn users that these substances are now controlled. Marketing claims about legal status are potentially misleading and legally risky.

#### Real Decreto 903/2025 (October 2025)

This legislation established a framework for medical cannabis preparations in Spain, but with significant limitations:

**What Was Legalized:**
- Hospital-based preparation of standardized cannabis preparations
- Magistral formulas for specific medical indications
- AEMPS-registered cannabis-based medicines

**What Was NOT Legalized:**
- Broad retail access to medical cannabis
- Dispensary-style distribution
- General patient access outside clinical settings

**Platform Implication:** Medical cannabis in Spain remains a clinical, controlled pathway. Tourist understanding that "Spain legalized medical weed" is incorrect and must be addressed in educational content.

### 2.4 Enforcement Landscape

#### 2024-2025 Enforcement Actions

Barcelona City Council has intensified actions against cannabis establishments during this period:

**June 2025 Operations:**
- 17 grow shops inspected in coordinated operation
- Confiscations of products with illegal labeling/advertising
- Administrative reports for regulatory violations
- Focus on cannabinoid products (CBD, HHC, THC variants)

**Club Closures:**
Multiple clubs have faced closure or sealing orders during this period, often related to:
- Neighbor complaints about noise or foot traffic
- Visibility that attracted regulatory attention
- Membership practices that appeared open rather than private
- Association with other establishments (restaurants, bars)

**Current Climate:**
The regulatory environment is dynamic and increasingly conservative. Past tolerance does not guarantee future tolerance. Platforms must emphasize current compliance and conservative behavior.

## 3. THE CANNABIS SOCIAL CLUB MODEL

### 3.1 Foundational Concept

#### Non-Profit Association Structure

Cannabis social clubs in Barcelona operate as private, non-profit associations under Spanish law. This structure is essential to their legal positioning:

**Core Principles:**
- Members collectively fund cannabis cultivation through donations
- No individual profits from cannabis distribution
- Governance through member meetings and elected boards
- Club exists to serve member needs, not commercial interests

**Practical Implications:**
- "Donations" replace retail "prices" (linguistic distinction matters legally)
- Members share cultivation costs proportional to consumption
- Club maintains member records and governance documentation
- Annual general meetings and financial transparency are standard

#### Comparison with Other Models

| Feature | Barcelona Clubs | Amsterdam Coffeeshops | California Dispensaries |
|---------|------------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Legal Status | Grey zone (tolerated) | Semi-legal | Fully legal (state) |
| Membership | Required (often vetted) | Open to tourists | Medical/recreational license |
| Product Access | Members only | Open to tourists | License holders |
| Public Consumption | Illegal | Designated areas | Legal (some restrictions) |
| Advertising | Minimal/restricted | Restricted | Regulated |

### 3.2 Membership Model

#### Standard Requirements

Most Barcelona cannabis social clubs maintain similar membership requirements:

**Age Verification:**
- Minimum 18 years old (federal legal limit)
- Many clubs self-regulate to 21+ as policy
- Government-issued photo ID required (passport, DNI, driver license)

**Residency Requirements:**
- Some clubs require Barcelona or Catalonia residency
- Others accept Spanish residency more broadly
- Tourist-friendly clubs exist but are increasingly scrutinized

**Membership Process:**
1. Initial inquiry through official channels (website, email, in-person)
2. Submission of identification documents
3. Completion of membership application/questionnaire
4. Payment of membership fee (typically €10–30 annually)
5. Review/approval period (hours to days, varies by club)
6. Formal membership confirmation

#### The "Invitation" Concept

Many clubs reference invitations or sponsorship, but this typically means:
- A current member can recommend an applicant
- Online inquiry through official channels is usually sufficient
- Clubs maintain discretion about their membership criteria
- "Invitations" from strangers online are almost certainly scams

### 3.3 Operational Structure

#### Typical Club Layout

**Reception/Admin Area:**
- ID verification
- Membership processing
- House rules briefing
- Fee collection

**Dispensary/Bar:**
- Menu display (typically digital or paper list)
- Product selection with budtender assistance
- Donation/payment processing
- Equipment provision

**Consumption Lounge:**
- Seating areas (varied configurations)
- Provided equipment (grinders, papers, bongs, dab rigs, vaporizers)
- Sometimes: entertainment (TVs, games, music)
- Sometimes: work spaces, WiFi

#### House Rules (Standard)

Almost all clubs enforce similar rules:

**Mandatory Compliance:**
- No photography or recording
- No mobile phone calls in consumption areas
- No dealing or distribution to non-members
- No bringing outside cannabis products
- No visible intoxication or disruptive behavior
- Membership card required for each visit

**Strongly Encouraged:**
- Discretion entering and exiting
- Respect for staff and fellow members
- Awareness of personal consumption limits
- Proper equipment handling and return

## 4. VISITOR ONBOARDING GUIDE

### 4.1 Pre-Visit Preparation

#### Essential Documents

Before attempting to visit any cannabis social club, visitors must have:

**Valid Identification:**
- Passport (any nationality)
- Spanish DNI (Documento Nacional de Identidad)
- EU driver license with photo
- National identity card from EU/EEA country

**Not Accepted:**
- Student cards (any country)
- Work permits alone
- Photocopies or digital copies (usually)
- Expired identification

**Financial Preparation:**
- Cash (many clubs are cash-only)
- €20–50 for initial membership and first donation
- Small bills preferred

#### Research Before Arrival

**Club Selection:**
- Review official websites (not social media promotions)
- Read recent reviews from credible sources
- Consider location relative to accommodation
- Note opening hours and peak times

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't trust Telegram groups offering "access"
- Don't pay strangers online for "invitations"
- Don't believe Instagram ads for clubs
- Don't follow random Reddit DMs promising access

### 4.2 The First Visit

#### Arrival Protocol

**At the Door:**
1. Approach reception confidently
2. State clearly: "I'd like to inquire about membership"
3. Present identification
4. Answer questions honestly about your situation

**Sample Script (from community advice):**
> "Hello, I'm visiting Barcelona for [X days/weeks]. I'm already a member of [other club/country] and I'd like to learn about your membership options. I have my passport with me."

**Registration Process:**
1. Complete membership application form
2. Provide estimated monthly consumption (standard question)
3. Pay annual membership fee
4. Receive membership confirmation (card or digital)
5. Receive house rules briefing
6. Tour of facilities (typically)

#### What to Expect Inside

**At the Dispensary:**
- Budtender will guide you through available products
- Products typically listed by with celar visuals
- Questions about your preferences are welcome
- Start with small amounts if unsure

**Equipment Provided:**
- Grinders
- Rolling papers and tips
- trays
- Water bongs
- Dab rigs
- Vaporizers (often Volcano-style)
- Ashtrays

**Atmosphere:**
- Varies by club (social vs. functional)
- Typically relaxed, respectful environment
- Staff available for guidance
- Other members generally respectful of privacy

### 4.3 Common Visitor Questions

#### "I'm only here for a weekend—can I join?"

**Answer:** Yes, many clubs accommodate short-term visitors. However:
- Some clubs prefer longer-term members
- Ask about minimum donation requirements
- Pre-rolls are often available for light users
- Be honest about your timeline

#### "Do I need to bring my own equipment?"

**Answer:** No, reputable clubs provide:
- Grinders, papers, tips, trays
- Smoking accessories (bongs, pipes)
- Vaporizers (high-quality units often provided)
- Everything you need for consumption

**Exception:** Personal preference items (favorite grinder, specific papers) are welcome but not required.

#### "How much should I get?"

**Answer:** Start small, especially if unfamiliar with the product or environment:
- 1–2 grams of flower is reasonable for a first visit
- Pre-rolls offer controlled dosing
- Staff can advise based on your experience
- You can return if you need more

#### "What if I don't like it?"

**Answer:** Most clubs:
- Won't refund membership fees
- Will allow you to leave without additional purchase
- Appreciate honest feedback
- Won't pressure you to buy more

## 5. SAFETY & HARM REDUCTION

### 5.1 General Safety Principles

#### Know Your Limits

**Fundamental Rule:** Start low, go slow. This is non-negotiable for new products and environments.

**Risk Factors That Increase Sensitivity:**
- Jet lag and fatigue
- Empty stomach
- Alcohol consumption
- Anxiety or stress
- New environment
- Unfamiliar product potency

#### Environment Awareness

**Inside the Club:**
- Know where exits are located
- Stay aware of your belongings
- Don't leave drinks unattended
- Ask staff if you feel unwell

**Outside the Club:**
- Public consumption is illegal and risky
- Remain discreet exiting the premises
- Don't display club merchandise or materials
- Don't discuss club membership in public spaces

### 5.2 Product-Specific Safety

#### Flower (Bud)

**Visual Quality Indicators:**
- Healthy green color (not yellow or brown)
- Visible trichomes (glittery appearance)
- Fresh smell (not musty or chemical)
- Proper cure (not wet or extremely dry)

**Sensory Red Flags:**
- Unusual chemical smell (potential pesticide contamination)
- Harsh taste beyond normal
- Headache or dizziness beyond expected high
- "Candy" or artificial smell (possible contamination)

**Safe Consumption:**
- Use provided equipment when possible
- Start with one inhalation, wait 15 minutes
- Don't mix with alcohol if uncertain
- Have water and food available

#### Edibles

**Highest Risk Category:** Edibles present the most common cause of adverse reactions.

**Why Edibles Are Risky:**
- Delayed onset (30 minutes to 2 hours)
- Easy to overconsume (eating more before first dose takes effect)
- Labeling is often unreliable
- Potency varies significantly

**Safe Edible Practices:**
- Assume labels are inaccurate (start with half or less)
- Wait at least 2 hours before considering more
- Don't consume on empty stomach
- Avoid mixing with alcohol
- Have a sober person present if possible

**What to Do If You've Had Too Much:**
- Stay calm (you will be okay)
- Hydrate with water or juice
- Eat something light
- Rest in comfortable, familiar environment
- Don't panic—distress worsens the experience
- Symptoms typically peak within 2–4 hours

#### Vaporizers and Cartridges

**Specific Risks:**
- Counterfeit/knockoff cartridges are common
- Unknown cutting agents and additives
- Battery and hardware issues
- Inconsistent potency

**Safety Guidelines:**
- Prefer cartridges from known sources
- Inspect packaging for authenticity markers
- Start with one small inhalation
- Don't use damaged or leaking cartridges
- Avoid "too good to be true" prices

#### Concentrates (Hash, Rosin, Wax)

**Higher Potency = Higher Risk:**
- THC content often 2–5x flower
- Easier to consume too much
- Physical effects can be overwhelming
- Require specific equipment for safe use

**Safe Concentrate Practices:**
- Use provided equipment (dab rigs, e-nails)
- Start with the smallest possible amount
- Don't combine with flower or alcohol initially
- Have experience with flower first before concentrates

### 5.3 Emergency Response

#### Recognizing Medical Emergencies

**Seek Immediate Help If:**
- Chest pain or heart palpitations
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe anxiety with physical symptoms (hyperventilation)
- Seizures
- Loss of consciousness
- Persistent vomiting
- Confusion or disorientation lasting more than several hours

**What to Do:**
1. Tell someone how you feel (buddy system)
2. Club staff are trained in basic response
3. Call 112 (Spanish emergency number) if severe
4. Don't leave the person alone
5. Keep environment calm and quiet

#### Harm Reduction Resources

**Energy Control:**
Spanish harm-reduction organization with Barcelona presence
- Education services
- Product analysis (where available)
- Confidential support

**Medical Resources:**
- Hospital emergency rooms (Urgencias)
- CAP (Centro de Atención Primaria) for non-emergencies
- 112 for emergencies

## 6. SCAM AWARENESS & PREVENTION

### 6.1 Common Scam Patterns

#### Reddit/Telegram DM Scams

**The Most Persistent Threat:** Unsolicited private messages offering cannabis access.

**How These Scams Work:**
1. You post a question about clubs or access
2. Scammer contacts you via DM (often after seeing your post)
3. They offer to "invite" you or connect you with a "plug"
4. They request payment (often crypto, PayPal, or gift cards)
5. You pay, receive nothing, and they block you

**Warning Signs:**
- Unsolicited DM offering cannabis access
- Request for payment before meeting
- Pressure tactics ("offer ends soon")
- Requests for gift cards or cryptocurrency
- "Girl" profile pictures (common scammer tactic)
- Any mention of Telegram for transactions
- Promise of "special rates" or "VIP access"

**Golden Rule:** Never send money to strangers online for cannabis access. Ever.

#### Fake Club Representations

**Social Media Scams:**
- Instagram accounts posing as clubs
- Facebook pages with fake reviews
- TikTok videos promoting "weed tourism"
- These often lead to:
  - Fake membership payments
  - Robbery when you arrive at "meetups"
  - Arrest scenarios (scammer working with police)

**Warning Signs:**
- Aggressive marketing on social media
- Promises of easy access for tourists
- Requests for payment via social media
- Club doesn't have a verifiable physical address
- No official website or the website looks unprofessional

#### Fake Product Scams

**In-Club Context:**
- Some "cannabis" products in Barcelona contain synthetic cannabinoids
- These can cause severe adverse reactions
- Not the same as HHC (now controlled) but more dangerous substances

**Warning Signs:**
- Unusually low prices
- Products with suspicious packaging
- "Weed" that smells like chemicals or candy
- Effects that feel "different" or wrong
- Staff who can't or won't answer questions about products

### 6.2 Club Selection Safety Checklist

#### Red Flags (Avoid These Clubs)

| Red Flag | Why It's Risky |
|----------|---------------|
| Actively markets to tourists | Attracts enforcement attention |
| No membership process | Likely illegal operation |
| Requests payment before seeing facility | Common scam pattern |
| Located near schools or parks | Police focus areas |
| No clear physical address | Scam indicator |
| Staff can't answer questions | Poor operation |
| Members behave aggressively | Bad community |
| Requires "deposit" | Often a scam |
| Offers home delivery | Always illegal in Spain |

#### Green Flags (Good Signs)

| Green Flag | Why It's Good |
|------------|---------------|
| Professional website with clear info | Legitimate operation |
| Clear membership process | Following regulations |
| Staff happy to explain rules | Good management |
| Clean, maintained facilities | Quality operation |
| Long-term member base | Sustainable club |
| Community recommendations | Social proof |
| Clear house rules | Professional management |
| Cash-only policy | Tax compliance indicator |

### 6.3 Digital Hygiene

#### Protecting Your Information

**Social Media:**
- Don't post photos of cannabis or clubs
- Don't mention club names publicly
- Use separate accounts if you post about cannabis
- Review privacy settings regularly

**Communication:**
- Use encrypted messaging for sensitive discussions
- Don't share personal documents with strangers
- Be wary of "friend" requests from new accounts
- Verify identities before sharing information

**Travel Security:**
- Don't store cannabis on your person in public
- Don't discuss cannabis with strangers
- Keep accommodation cannabis in private space
- Know your rights if stopped by police

## 7. PRODUCT KNOWLEDGE

### 7.1 Product Categories

#### Flower (Weed, Bud)

**Types Commonly Available:**
- Sativa strains (energizing, cerebral effects)
- Indica strains (relaxing, body effects)
- Hybrid strains (balanced effects)
- Auto-flowering varieties
- CBD-dominant strains (low THC)

**Quality Indicators:**
- Trichome coverage (visible crystals)
- Proper cure (not too wet, not too dry)
- Aroma profile (varies by strain)
- No seeds, stems, or contaminants
- Consistent density

**Common Pricing:**
- €10–15 per gram (typical range)
- Higher for premium strains
- Often sold in 1g, 2g, 3.5g units
- Pre-rolls typically €5–10 each

#### Hash (Hashish)

**Types Available:**
- Traditional pressed hash (Moroccan, Afghan styles)
- Bubble hash (water-extracted)
- Dry sift hash
- Rosin (solventless extract)

**Quality Indicators:**
- Color consistency (golden to dark brown)
- Texture (pliable to brittle, depends on type)
- Aroma (earthy, spicy, not chemical)
- Burns cleanly when heated

**Consumption Methods:**
- Added to flower in joints
- Vaporized (especially bubble hash/rosin)
- Used in dab rigs (rosin, high-quality hash)

#### Extracts and Concentrates

**Types Available:**
- Shatter, wax, budder (solvent extracts)
- Rosin (solventless)
- Live resin (fresh frozen material)
- Hash oil (various extraction methods)

**Potency Considerations:**
- THC content often 50–80%+ (vs. 15–25% flower)
- Effects hit faster and harder
- Easier to overconsume
- Require specific equipment

**Safety with Extracts:**
- Use proper dabbing equipment
- Temperature control matters
- Start with rice-grain-sized amount
- Don't mix with other products initially

#### Vape Products

**Types Available:**
- Disposable vape pens
- Refillable cartridge systems
- 510-thread batteries and cartridges
- Dry herb vaporizers

**Product Quality Concerns:**
- Counterfeit cartridges are widespread
- Additives and cutting agents common
- Battery safety varies
- Lab testing rarely available

**What to Check:**
- Cartridge packaging integrity
- Brand recognition (if any)
- Price (suspiciously cheap = suspicious)
- Staff knowledge of sourcing

#### Edibles (Limited Availability)

**Types Sometimes Available:**
- Gummies and sweets
- Brownies and baked goods
- Chocolates
- Beverages

**Important Notes:**
- Availability has decreased due to enforcement
- Dosing is often unreliable
- Some products may contain novel cannabinoids
- Local production = variable quality

**Consumer Guidance:**
- Assume potency labels are inaccurate
- Start with 2.5mg THC or less
- Wait 2+ hours before re-dosing
- Avoid if anxious about effects

### 7.2 Understanding Strain Effects

#### Sativa vs. Indica (Traditional Classification)

**Sativa Effects:**
- Energizing and uplifting
- Cerebral and creative
- Daytime use preferred
- May increase anxiety in some users

**Indica Effects:**
- Relaxing and sedating
- Body-focused ("couch lock" potential)
- Evening/night use preferred
- May cause drowsiness

**Important Caveat:**
Modern cannabis breeding has blurred these lines significantly. Most strains are hybrids with mixed characteristics. Individual reaction varies based on:
- Personal tolerance
- Setting and mindset
- Product quality and potency
- Consumption method

#### THC vs. CBD

**THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol):**
- Primary psychoactive compound
- Effects: euphoria, relaxation, appetite, altered perception
- Risks: anxiety, paranoia, cognitive impairment, nausea

**CBD (Cannabidiol):**
- Non-psychoactive (doesn't cause "high")
- Effects: relaxation, anti-inflammatory, anxiety reduction
- Often used for medical purposes
- Can moderate THC effects

**Ratio Products:**
- High THC, low CBD: Strong psychoactive effects
- Balanced (1:1): Moderate effects with therapeutic benefits
- High CBD, low THC: Minimal high, therapeutic benefits

### 7.3 Novel Cannabinoids (2025 Update)

#### What's Changed

**Orden SND/380/2025** brought multiple novel cannabinoids under control:

**Previously Available, Now Controlled:**
- HHC (Hexahydrocannabinol)
- HHC-O (HHC acetate)
- THCP and variants
- THC-O (THC acetate)

**Why This Matters:**
- These products may still exist in circulation
- Possession and sale are now legally risky
- Products marketed as "legal THC alternatives" are misleading
- Some may still appear in clubs or retail settings

**Consumer Guidance:**
- Avoid products claiming to be "legal" alternatives
- Stick to traditional THC products from known sources
- Be skeptical of products with unusual effects or packaging
- Ask staff explicitly about product contents

## 8. CLUB ETIQUETTE & CULTURE

### 8.1 Inside the Club

#### Arrival and Registration

**At Reception:**
- Have membership card ready
- Sign in if required
- Follow staff instructions
- Don't rush the process

**First Impressions Matter:**
- Be polite to staff
- Answer questions honestly
- Show respect for the process
- Don't demand special treatment

#### The Dispensary (Bar)

**Approaching Staff:**
- Wait your turn
- Ask questions if unsure
- Don't pressure for recommendations
- Accept that not all products may be available

**Making Selections:**
- Staff are there to help, not just sell
- Describe your experience level honestly
- Ask about effects, not just strain names
- Start small if new

**Payment:**
- Many clubs are cash-only
- Donations, not purchases (terminology matters)
- Tipping is appreciated but not required
- Keep receipts if you want records

#### Consumption Areas

**General Conduct:**
- Use equipment properly (ask if unsure)
- Clean up after yourself
- Don't touch other members' belongings
- Respect personal space

**Social Etiquette:**
- Conversation is welcome but keep it appropriate
- Music, TV volume—be aware of others
- Don't disrupt people enjoying quiet time
- Being "too much" is universally frowned upon

**Photography:**
- Strictly prohibited in most clubs
- Phone calls in consumption areas often discouraged
- Respect this rule absolutely
- Violations can get you banned

### 8.2 Member Responsibilities

#### Community Participation

**What Good Members Do:**
- Follow house rules consistently
- Respect staff and fellow members
- Provide feedback constructively
- Help new members when appropriate
- Maintain confidentiality

**What Good Members DON'T Do:**
- Bring non-members (ever)
- Share products outside the club
- Behave aggressively or rudely
- Make scenes or drama
- Discuss club matters publicly

#### Protecting the Community

**Confidentiality:**
- What happens in the club stays in the club
- Don't post about clubs on social media
- Don't share member names or identities
- Don't share staff names or information

**Reporting Issues:**
- Report problems to staff immediately
- Document concerns if they persist
- Support good management decisions
- Help maintain standards

### 8.3 Common Etiquette Mistakes

#### Tourist-Specific Issues

**Behaviors That Damage the Scene:**
- Loud, drunken behavior
- Treating staff like servants
- Complaining about prices (donations)
- Taking phone calls in consumption areas
- Demanding special treatment
- Photographing everything
- Being visibly intoxicated before entering

**Cultural Sensitivity:**
- Learn basic Spanish greetings
- Respect Catalan culture and language
- Be aware of local norms
- Don't be "that loud American/Brit/German"

#### Newcomer Mistakes

**Common Errors:**
- Consuming too much too fast
- Mixing with alcohol immediately
- Asking too many questions at peak times
- Being nervous (it's okay, we all were)
- Comparing to Amsterdam (offensive to locals)

**How to Avoid Looking Like a Fool:**
- Observe first, then participate
- Ask staff for guidance, not demands
- Start slow
- Be honest about your experience

### Module 3: Scam Awareness Guide

**Full Scam Prevention Module**

#### Section 1: The Golden Rule

```
NEVER SEND MONEY TO STRANGERS ONLINE FOR CANNABIS ACCESS.

This applies to:
✗ Reddit DMs offering "connections"
✗ Telegram accounts selling "memberships"
✗ Instagram "clubs" requesting payment
✗ Anyone who contacts YOU first
✗ "Friends of friends" recommendations
✗ Deposit requirements before seeing anything

Real clubs don't need to recruit strangers.
Real clubs have official channels.
Real clubs won't DM you first.

If someone contacts you offering cannabis access,
assume it's a scam until proven otherwise.
```

#### Section 2: Common Scam Patterns

**Pattern 1: The DM Invitation**

```
SCENARIO:
You post about visiting Barcelona.
A friendly stranger DMs: "I can help you get into a club."

RED FLAGS:
• They contacted you first
• They want payment upfront
• They use Telegram/WhatsApp
• They ask for gift cards or crypto
• They promise special access
• Their profile seems fake

OUTCOME:
You pay. They block you. You lose money.

CORRECT ACTION:
Ignore and block.
Use official club channels only.
```

**Pattern 2: The Instagram Club**

```
SCENARIO:
You find an Instagram account for a "Barcelona Cannabis Club."
They post beautiful photos and ask you to DM for membership.

RED FLAGS:
• Aggressive social media marketing
• Asking for payment via social media
• No official website
• Can't verify physical location
• "Easy tourist access" messaging

OUTCOME:
You pay. They either rob you or nothing exists.

CORRECT ACTION:
Research official websites.
Look for community recommendations.
Verify physical location.
```

**Pattern 3: The Telegram Plug**

```
SCENARIO:
Someone on Telegram offers to sell you cannabis.
They claim to be a "club contact" or "member."

RED FLAGS:
• Selling cannabis is illegal in Spain
• Club membership doesn't authorize dealing
• They want to meet in public
• They ask for money upfront

OUTCOME:
You could be scammed or arrested.

CORRECT ACTION:
Use clubs only for cannabis access.
Never buy from individuals.
```

#### Section 3: Verification Checklist

```
BEFORE ENGAGING WITH ANY "ACCESS" OFFER:

□ Did they contact me first? → SCAM if yes
□ Are they asking for payment? → RED FLAG
□ Are they using Telegram/WhatsApp? → RED FLAG
□ Can I verify they represent a real club? → Required
□ Does the club have an official website? → Required
□ Can I find independent reviews? → Required
□ Does it sound too easy? → USUALLY SCAM

IF ANY RED FLAG: WALK AWAY.
```

### 11.2 Frequently Asked Questions (Community Verified)

#### Legal Questions

**Q: Is cannabis legal in Barcelona?**
A: No, but personal use and private cultivation are decriminalized. Public consumption remains illegal. Cannabis clubs exist in a legal grey zone.

**Q: Can tourists join clubs?**
A: Some clubs accept tourists, but it's becoming more difficult. Many clubs prefer residents. Tourist-friendly clubs may have additional requirements.

**Q: What happens if I'm caught with cannabis in public?**
A: Typically a fine (€600–30,000) and product confiscation. Arrest is possible in aggravated circumstances.

**Q: Can I fly with cannabis to/from Barcelona?**
A: No. This is international drug trafficking and carries severe penalties. Never transport cannabis across borders.

#### Practical Questions

**Q: Do I need cash?**
A: Yes, most clubs are cash-only.

**Q: What ID do I need?**
A: Valid government photo ID (passport, DNI, driver license). Student cards are not accepted.

**Q: How much does membership cost?**
A: Typically €10–30 annually. Some clubs have additional fees.

**Q: Can I bring a friend?**
A: No. Only members can enter. Attempting to bring guests is grounds for membership revocation.

**Q: How much cannabis can I get?**
A: This varies by club and membership. Typically there's a monthly limit based on "personal use" norms. Short visits can usually get 1–3 grams.

#### Safety Questions

**Q: Is the product safe?**
A: Quality varies by club. Ask questions, start slow. Some clubs test products, most don't. Always exercise caution.

**Q: What about synthetic cannabinoids?**
A: These appear occasionally and can be dangerous. If product effects feel "wrong," stop consuming and seek help.

**Q: What if I have a bad reaction?**
A: Club staff should be trained in basic response. Call 112 for emergencies. Stay calm, hydrate, rest.
