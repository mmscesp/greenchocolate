<AnswerBox>
  In a medical emergency, **call 112 immediately.** Cannabis overdose is not fatal, but adverse reactions can be scary. In a police encounter, **remain calm, be respectful, and exercise your right to silence.**
</AnswerBox>

## Medical Emergencies

**Call 112 (General Emergency)** or go to the nearest hospital Emergency Room (Urgencias).

**Signs you need help:**
*   Chest pain or heart palpitations.
*   Severe anxiety/panic attack.
*   Persistent vomiting.
*   Difficulty breathing.

**Energy Control Barcelona:**
*   A harm reduction organization offering non-judgmental support.
*   **Location:** C/ de l'Aurora, 27, 08001 Barcelona
*   **Hours:** Check their website for current hours.
*   **Services:** Anonymous drug checking (when available), counseling, safe use information.

## Police Encounters

If you are stopped by police (Guardia Urbana or Mossos d'Esquadra):

1.  **Stay Calm:** Aggression will escalate the situation.
2.  **ID:** You are required to show identification (Passport, DNI).
3.  **Right to Silence:** You are not required to answer questions about where you are going or what you have been doing. Politely say: *"No tengo nada que declarar"* (I have nothing to declare).
4.  **Fines:** If issued a fine for public possession (€601-€30,000), accept the ticket. You can contest it later with a lawyer.
5.  **No Signatures:** Do not sign any documents you do not fully understand.

<WarningBox title="Never Do This">
  *   Do not attempt to bribe the police. This is a serious crime in Spain.
  *   Do not run. This makes you look guilty.
  *   Do not consent to a search unless they have a warrant (rare for simple stops).
</WarningBox>

## Tourist Assistance

**Your Embassy:**
*   Can provide a list of English-speaking lawyers.
*   Cannot get you out of jail or pay your fines.

**24h Pharmacies:**
*   Look for a green cross. Staff can help with minor medical issues.
