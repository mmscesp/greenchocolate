## Why This Guide Exists

Visitors face the same problem again and again: there is very little clear public information, and the gap gets filled by scammers, outdated advice, and unrealistic expectations. The goal of this guide is to make your behavior slower, safer, and harder to exploit.

## Before You Leave Your Hotel

Prepare before you move, not while you are already in the street.

- Research clubs in advance
- Confirm whether they accept visitors
- Check what identification they require
- Save the details offline in case your phone signal or battery fails

Our [directory](/en/clubs/) is the right place to start if you need verified basics.

## Getting There

The number one rule is simple: never let a stranger redirect your plan.

Street promoters, random invitations, and hand-delivered cards are part of the risk layer, not part of the club system.

- Do not follow a promoter
- Do not accept a flyer as proof of legitimacy
- Use your own map and your own verified address

## At the Door

Be direct, calm, and honest.

- Say who you are
- Mention your membership or prior application clearly
- Have your ID ready
- Accept that there may be a wait, paperwork, or even a refusal

Arguing at the entrance is one of the fastest ways to end the interaction badly.

## Inside the Club

Privacy and restraint matter more than performance.

- Keep your phone away
- Do not take photos or videos
- Do not interrogate other members
- Listen to staff and follow house rules the first time

## Absolute Red Lines

- Do not take anything outside the club unless you fully understand the risk
- Do not consume in public
- Do not share your membership with others
- Do not publish club locations or details online

Public possession or public consumption can still lead to administrative fines. If you need the legal baseline, read [Spain's Cannabis Laws for Tourists](/en/editorial/spain-cannabis-laws-tourists).

## Red Flags That Mean Leave

- no clear intake process
- no membership documentation
- money demanded before anything is explained
- a vague apartment, car, or improvised location
- anyone telling you not to worry about rules

## If Something Goes Wrong

Stay calm and reduce exposure first.

- Leave if the place feels unsafe
- Do not argue in the street
- Call **112** in a real emergency
- Treat any demand for on-the-spot cash by random people as a scam

For emergencies and police encounters, see [Emergency Resources](/en/editorial/emergency-resources).

## Save This Checklist

Before:

- Club verified
- Application or membership confirmed
- ID ready
- Details saved offline

During:

- Phone away
- No photos
- Full respect for house rules

After:

- No public consumption
- No location sharing
- No club details on social media
