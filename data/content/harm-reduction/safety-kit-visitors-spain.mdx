### Why This Guide Exists

Spain's cannabis club scene has an information problem, and that problem puts visitors at risk every single day.

There is no official guide. No tourist board FAQ. No government resource. The people who know how things work are inside the clubs, and they cannot speak publicly. The people who show up without preparation are the ones who get scammed, fined, or worse.

---

### Before You Leave Your Hotel

**Research clubs in advance.** Never wing it. Identify clubs that accept visitors, confirm their requirements, and initiate contact before you arrive in the city. Our [directory](/en/clubs/) shows which verified clubs accept international visitors, response times, and language support.

**Have your ID ready.** Passport for non-Spanish visitors. DNI or NIE for residents. Every legitimate club will require government-issued photo identification.

**Know the club's specific requirements before you go.** Some require a referral. Some require advance application. Some have minimum age requirements above 18.

**Save the details offline.** Screenshot the club's verified details and keep them available when you need them.

---

### Getting There

**Never follow a street promoter.** This is the number one rule.

Street promoters are not affiliated with legitimate clubs. They profit from confusion and often route visitors toward scams.

**Never accept flyers, cards, or invitations from strangers.** Legitimate clubs do not recruit on the street.

**Use maps, not people, to find your way.** If you have the address from a verified source, navigate there yourself.

---

### At the Door

**Be honest about who you are.** Tourist. First-timer. Visiting from abroad.

**Mention your membership or referral clearly.** If you applied in advance, show confirmation immediately.

**Be patient.** Intake can include wait time, paperwork, and rule briefing.

**Accept rejection gracefully.** Clubs can decline applications. Do not argue.

---

### Inside the Club

**No photos. No videos. No stories. No check-ins.**

**Do not ask other members personal questions.** Privacy is fundamental.

**Keep your voice down and follow house rules.** Staff decisions are final.

---

### What NOT to Do - The Absolute Lines

**Do not take anything outside the club.**

**Do not consume in public.** Public consumption or possession is an administrative offense in Spain, with fines from EUR601 to EUR30,000. Read the [public vs private guide](/en/editorial/public-vs-private-cannabis-spain).

**Do not share your membership with others.**

**Do not post club location details online.**

---

### Red Flags - Walk Away If You See Any of These

- No intake process
- No membership documentation
- Cash demanded before entry
- The place is an apartment, car, or unclear location
- You are told not to worry about rules

---

### If Something Goes Wrong

Stay calm. Leave immediately if you feel unsafe. Do not argue.

Emergency number in Spain: **112**.

If you receive a fine from police, it comes with official documentation. If someone asks for cash on the street, it is a scam. See [what to do if you get a fine](/en/editorial/cannabis-fine-spain-what-to-do).

### The Checklist (Save This)

Before you go:
- Club researched and verified
- Membership/application confirmed
- ID ready
- Club details saved offline

At the club:
- Phone in pocket
- No photos
- Respect house rules

After you leave:
- No public consumption
- No location sharing
- No social media details
