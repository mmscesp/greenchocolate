<AnswerBox>
  Barcelona's clubs are private communities, not retail stores. The biggest mistakes tourists make are treating staff like waiters, trying to negotiate "prices" (they are donations), and taking photos inside the club. Respect the privacy and the non-profit nature of the association.
</AnswerBox>

## Mistake #1: Treating it Like a Shop
In a shop, the customer is always right. In a Social Club, you are a **member of an association**. The staff are often volunteers or board members.
*   **Don't** say "How much is this weed?"
*   **Do** say "What is the donation for this strain?" or "Can I contribute for this?"

## Mistake #2: Taking Photos
Privacy is the #1 rule. Many members (locals) have jobs and families and rely on the club's discretion.
*   **The Rule:** Phones away. No photos of the interior, the menu, or other members.
*   **The Consequence:** You will likely be banned immediately.

<WarningBox title="Privacy First">
  If you pull out your phone to snap a pic for Instagram, expect to be asked to leave. Clubs operate in a legal grey zone; evidence of their operation (like photos) puts them at risk.
</WarningBox>

## Mistake #3: Asking for "To Go" Cups
This isn't Starbucks. While you can take your dispensing with you (concealed), the club is a social space meant for consumption *on-site*.
*   Asking for "takeaway" implies you are buying product to distribute or consume publicly, which looks like trafficking.
*   **Best Practice:** Chill for a bit. Consume some there. Be part of the community.

## Mistake #4: Being Loud Outside
Clubs often close because of **neighborhood complaints**.
*   Don't loiter outside the door.
*   Don't smoke immediately after leaving.
*   Keep your voice down in the street, especially at night.

## Mistake #5: Trying to Negotiate
Membership fees and dispensing contributions are fixed to cover costs. Attempting to haggle is insulting and signals you think this is a black market deal. It's not. It's a collective.
