## Set Your Expectations First

Your first visit is not a casual walk-in errand. It may take planning, waiting, and a little humility. Access is not guaranteed, and the clubs that do things properly will often move more slowly than tourists expect.

## Step 1: Research Before You Fly

Do this before arrival, not on the same afternoon you want to go.

- Identify clubs that accept international visitors
- Check application requirements and response times
- Confirm language support
- Keep backup options

Start with [the directory](/en/clubs/).

## Step 2: Apply Through Verified Channels

Use official or verified channels only. Do not rely on social-media strangers, street promoters, or anyone offering a shortcut.

Send accurate information, wait for a formal response, and understand that a rejection is possible. That is not rude. It is part of controlled access.

## Step 3: Prepare Properly for the Visit

Bring what you actually need:

- valid government-issued ID
- membership confirmation or prior approval
- cash if the club requires it

Do not bring extra guests unless the club has explicitly said that is allowed.

## Step 4: Handle Arrival Like an Adult

When you arrive, let the intake process happen. That may include a wait, questions, paperwork, and a briefing on house rules.

Read everything you are given. If something is unclear, ask once and listen to the answer.

## Step 5: Respect the Space Once You Are Inside

The safest posture is quiet, observant, and low-drama.

- keep your phone away
- respect other members' privacy
- follow staff guidance
- do not behave like you are in a retail shop

You are inside a private association, not a public storefront.

## Step 6: Leave Without Creating New Risk

A good visit can still turn stupid in the final five minutes if you forget where you are.

- no public consumption
- no location sharing
- no posting club details on social media

The club's privacy does not end when you step outside.

## The Most Common First-Time Mistakes

- showing up without confirmed membership
- following strangers for access
- taking photos inside
- acting like Barcelona works like Amsterdam
- consuming publicly after leaving
