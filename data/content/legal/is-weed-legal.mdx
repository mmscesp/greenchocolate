<AnswerBox>
  **No, cannabis is not strictly "legal" in Barcelona.** It is decriminalized for private consumption but illegal in public. You cannot buy it in shops. You can only consume it in private spaces (like your home) or in private "Cannabis Social Clubs" where you are a registered member. Public possession or consumption carries fines starting at €601.
</AnswerBox>

## The "Grey Zone" Explained

Many tourists arrive in Barcelona expecting an Amsterdam-style "coffeeshop" experience. This is dangerous. Spain's laws are based on a distinction between **public** and **private** space.

### 1. Public Space = Strict Prohibition
If you are caught with cannabis in public (street, beach, park, car), you are committing a "serious administrative offense" under the *Ley Mordaza* (Gag Law).
*   **Penalty:** Fines from **€601 to €30,000**.
*   **Record:** No criminal record (it's administrative), but you must pay.
*   **Police Power:** Police can search you if they suspect you are carrying drugs.

### 2. Private Space = Tolerance
Consumption and cultivation in strictly private spaces (where it is not visible to the public) is generally protected by the right to privacy. This is the loophole that allows **Cannabis Social Clubs** to exist.

## What is a Cannabis Social Club?
These are **non-profit associations**, not businesses.
*   Members pool resources to cultivate cannabis for their own consumption.
*   There are no "sales." You pay a "membership fee" and contribute towards cultivation costs.
*   **Strictly Private:** You must be a member to enter.

<WarningBox title="Tourist Traps & Scams">
  Avoid street promoters who say "Come to my coffee shop!" or "Legal weed here!" Legitimate clubs **never** use street promoters. Following these people often leads to:
  *   Illegal "fake" clubs that get raided.
  *   Robbery or scamming.
  *   Low-quality, unregulated products.
</WarningBox>

## 2026 Update: What Changed?
In 2026, enforcement has tightened significantly.
1.  **Administrative Inspections:** The City Council is aggressively closing clubs that act like dispensaries (sales to tourists, advertising).
2.  **Novel Cannabinoids Banned:** HHC and other semi-synthetic cannabinoids were added to the controlled substance list. Products sold as "legal highs" are often now illegal or unregulated.
