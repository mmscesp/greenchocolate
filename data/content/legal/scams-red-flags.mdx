<AnswerBox>
  **99% of unsolicited offers are scams.** If a stranger messages you offering "access," they are trying to steal your money. Real clubs never use street promoters or random DMs.
</AnswerBox>

## The DM Invitation Scam

You post in a forum: "Visiting Barcelona, where can I find...?"

Within minutes, you get a private message: 
> "Hey! I know a great spot. Just send me €50 on PayPal and I'll send you the address."

**What happens next:**
1. You pay.
2. They block you.
3. You have no recourse.

**Why it's a scam:** Legitimate clubs are non-profit associations. They do not have "middlemen" collecting fees on the street.

## The Street Promoter

In tourist areas (La Rambla, Barceloneta), promoters stand on corners handing out cards or whispering "Hashish? Marijuana?"

<WarningBox title="Dangerous Territory">
  Street promoters are often police decoys. Even if they are not, they sell low-quality, overpriced product or lead you to fake clubs where you will be robbed.
</WarningBox>

**The Rule:** If it feels like a drug deal, walk away. Legal clubs are private associations behind closed doors, not street transactions.

## The Telegram "Menu" Scam

Fake Telegram accounts post daily menus with professional photos and cheap prices.
> "We deliver to your hotel! Order now!"

**This is 100% illegal.** Delivery is not permitted under Spanish law. These are either:
*   Police stings.
*   Scammers who take your money and disappear.
*   Gangs selling dangerous synthetic products.

## How to Stay Safe

**Only** use official channels:
1. **Official websites:** Look for proper domains (.com, .es), not social media accounts.
2. **Community references:** Ask established locals, not random strangers.
3. **Visit directly:** Go to the neighborhood, find the discreet door, and ring the bell.

If you are scammed, report it to the police (Mossos d'Esquadra). While cannabis exists in a grey zone, **theft is a crime**.
