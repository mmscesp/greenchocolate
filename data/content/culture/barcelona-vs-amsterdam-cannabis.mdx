### The Assumption That Gets People in Trouble

Tourists land in Barcelona expecting Amsterdam-style access: public listings, walk-ins, and instant service. That expectation is wrong in every detail.

---

### Amsterdam: How Coffeeshops Work

Amsterdam coffeeshops are publicly visible and publicly accessible under a tolerance framework.

- Walk in with ID
- Browse menu
- Purchase and consume under local rules

Tourists are part of the operating model.

---

### Spain: How Cannabis Social Clubs Work

Spain's CSCs are private associations, not public storefronts.

- No public walk-ins
- Membership required in advance
- No advertising model
- Access and rules vary by club

Tourists are not the default audience.

---

### Side by Side

| | Amsterdam Coffeeshop | Spain CSC |
|---|---|---|
| Walk-in access | Yes | No |
| Membership required | No | Yes |
| Public listing | Yes | No |
| Tourist model | Primary | Variable |
| Public consumption risk | Lower in context | High (fines possible) |

---

### Why the Difference Creates Danger

The expectation gap is where scams happen. If you search, approach, and behave as if Barcelona were Amsterdam, your risk goes up fast.
