## A Community-First Model

Spanish cannabis social clubs were created as private, non-profit associations. Instead of public retail sales, members collectively organize access and consume in private environments.

## Why the Model Expanded

Clubs became popular because they offered a safer social setting than street purchasing. Many associations also promoted harm reduction, community guidelines, and a lower-visibility model than open commercial storefronts.

## Why Legal Clarity Still Matters

Even with broad social adoption, clubs still operate inside a sensitive legal context. Public possession and public consumption remain sanctionable, and local enforcement can change quickly.

## What Visitors Should Understand

- Clubs are private associations, not public coffee shops.
- Membership checks and rules are part of compliance, not bureaucracy.
- Behavior outside the venue affects neighborhood trust and long-term viability.
